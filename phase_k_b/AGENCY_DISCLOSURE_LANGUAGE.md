## Purpose

This document defines canonical disclosure language for declared agency patterns.

All language is factual. No softening words. No justification. No persuasion. No recommendation tone.

---

## Disclosure Language Templates

### Template 1: Pre-Selection Disclosure

**Use Case**: Pre-selected option (DEFAULT_SELECTION)

**Exact Text**: "This option is pre-selected. This pre-selection influences attention and choice."

**Language Characteristics**:
- Factual statement of pre-selection state
- Explicit statement of influence on attention
- Explicit statement of influence on choice
- No justification words (because, since, to help)
- No recommendation words (recommended, suggested, best)
- No persuasion words (should, might want to, consider)
- No softening words (a bit, somewhat, slightly)

**Forbidden Language**:
- "This option is recommended"
- "We suggest this option"
- "This is the best choice"
- "This option is pre-selected to help you"
- "You might want to use this option"
- "This option is slightly emphasized"

## Language Principles

### Principle 1: Factual Statements Only

**Rule**: Disclosure language must state facts only.

**Forbidden**: Justifications, recommendations, suggestions, preferences, optimizations

**Example (Correct)**: "This option is pre-selected. This pre-selection influences attention and choice."

**Example (Forbidden)**: "This option is pre-selected because it's the most common choice."

---

### Principle 2: Explicit Influence Statement

**Rule**: Disclosure language must explicitly state that the mechanism influences attention or choice.

**Required Phrases**: "influences attention", "influences choice", or "influences attention and choice"

**Forbidden**: Implied influence, hidden influence, subtle influence

**Example (Correct)**: "This emphasis influences attention and choice."

**Example (Forbidden)**: "This element is visually emphasized."

---

### Principle 3: No Softening Language

**Rule**: Disclosure language must not use words that soften the statement of influence.

**Forbidden Words**: a bit, somewhat, slightly, may, might, could, tends to, usually

**Example (Correct)**: "This pre-selection influences attention and choice."

**Example (Forbidden)**: "This pre-selection might slightly influence your attention."

---

### Principle 4: No Justification Language

**Rule**: Disclosure language must not justify why the mechanism exists.

**Forbidden Phrases**: because, since, to help, to guide, to make it easier, for your convenience

**Example (Correct)**: "This option is pre-selected. This pre-selection influences attention and choice."

**Example (Forbidden)**: "This option is pre-selected to make it easier for you."

---

### Principle 5: No Recommendation Language

**Rule**: Disclosure language must not use recommendation words.

**Forbidden Words**: recommended, suggested, best, preferred, optimal, ideal

**Example (Correct)**: "This element is visually emphasized. This emphasis influences attention and choice."

**Example (Forbidden)**: "This element is recommended. This recommendation influences attention and choice."

## Canonical Phrases

### Required Phrases

**1. Pre-Selection State**
- "This option is pre-selected."

**2. Visual Emphasis State**
- "This element is visually emphasized."

**3. Influence Statement**
- "This [mechanism] influences attention and choice."

**4. Change Ability Statement**
- "You can change it."

---

### Forbidden Phrases

**1. Recommendation Phrases**
- "This option is recommended"
- "We suggest this option"
- "This is the best choice"

**2. Justification Phrases**
- "to help you"
- "to make it easier"
- "for your convenience"
- "to guide you"

**3. Softening Phrases**
- "might influence"
- "could influence"
- "slightly influences"
- "somewhat influences"

**4. Persuasion Phrases**
- "you might want to"
- "you should consider"
- "we recommend"

**Variation Rule**: No variations are permitted. Exact text must be used.

**Extension Rule**: No extensions are permitted. Additional text beyond canonical language is forbidden.
