## Prohibited Modifications

### Prohibition 1: Incremental Interpretive Modifications

**Statement**: Incremental interpretive modifications to frozen components are prohibited.

**Examples of Prohibited Modifications**:
- Adding explanatory text that re-interprets existing rules
- Softening language in existing constraints
- Expanding scope of existing classifications without new evidence
- Adding "clarifications" that modify rule meaning

---

### Prohibition 2: Rule Modifications Without New Phase

**Statement**: Modifications to governance rules (G-RULE-001 through G-RULE-012) are prohibited without entering Phase K-D or Phase L.

**Examples of Prohibited Modifications**:
- Changing rule trigger conditions
- Modifying required declarations
- Adjusting failure consequences
- Adding exceptions to rules

---

### Prohibition 3: Gate Modifications Without New Phase

**Statement**: Modifications to review gates (GATE-PRE-MERGE, GATE-PRE-RELEASE, GATE-POST-CHANGE-AUDIT) are prohibited without entering Phase K-D or Phase L.

**Examples of Prohibited Modifications**:
- Adding or removing gate checks
- Modifying approval criteria
- Changing blocking conditions
- Adding human override mechanisms

---

### Prohibition 4: Pattern Modifications Without New Phase

**Statement**: Modifications to declared agency patterns (PATTERN-DECLARED-DEFAULT-SELECTION, PATTERN-DECLARED-VISUAL-HIGHLIGHT, PATTERN-DECLARED-PRE-SELECTION-REJECTION) are prohibited without entering Phase K-D or Phase L.

**Examples of Prohibited Modifications**:
- Changing canonical disclosure language
- Modifying UI mechanism specifications
- Adjusting rejection mechanism requirements

### Operation 3: Evidence Addition
